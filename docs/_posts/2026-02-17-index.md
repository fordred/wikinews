#### Science and technology

* [Solar eclipse of February 17, 2026](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_February_17%2C_2026 "Solar eclipse of February 17, 2026")
  * An [annular solar eclipse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annular_solar_eclipse "Annular solar eclipse") lasting up to 2 minutes and 20 seconds is visible from [Antarctica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctica "Antarctica"), the [Southern Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Ocean "Southern Ocean"), and the [South Indian Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Indian_Ocean "South Indian Ocean"). It is the first eclipse on February 17 to occur since February 1897 lunar eclipse. Observers in south [Argentina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argentina "Argentina") and [Chile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chile "Chile"), as well as south [Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa "Africa") and [Madagascar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madagascar "Madagascar"), also see a partial eclipse. It is the last annular solar eclipse to be visible in the contigous Antarctica until [2061](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_October_13%2C_2061 "Solar eclipse of October 13, 2061").
