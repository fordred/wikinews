  * [2025 WTA Finals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_WTA_Finals "2025 WTA Finals")
    * In [tennis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tennis "Tennis"), [Elena Rybakina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elena_Rybakina "Elena Rybakina") of [Kazakhstan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sport_in_Kazakhstan "Sport in Kazakhstan") becomes the first Asian player to win a [WTA Finals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WTA_Finals "WTA Finals") title after defeating [Aryna Sabalenka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aryna_Sabalenka "Aryna Sabalenka"), 6–3, 7–6(7–0) to win the [singles title](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_WTA_Finals_%E2%80%93_Singles "2025 WTA Finals – Singles") at the [2025 WTA Finals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_WTA_Finals "2025 WTA Finals"). By winning the title undefeated, Rybakina wins [US$](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar "United States dollar")5.235 million in prize money, breaking the [previous year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_WTA_Finals "2024 WTA Finals")'s record for the largest prize money earned by a female tennis player at a single event. [(BBC Sports)](https://www.bbc.com/sport/tennis/articles/c33pl2j56vgo)
