[(AFP via CNA)](https://www.channelnewsasia.com/world/russia-china-lavrov-iran-war-energy-resources-6057471)
  * [Norway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norway "Norway") records its highest-ever [crude oil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crude_oil "Crude oil") [export](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_in_Norway "Energy in Norway") value in March, reaching 57.4 billion [kroner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norwegian_krone "Norwegian krone") ([US$](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar "United States dollar")6.08 billion) for 56.6 million barrels, contributing to a [trade surplus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade_surplus "Trade surplus") of 97.5 billion kroner ($10.3 billion) amid a global fuel crisis. [(Bloomberg)](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-15/norway-s-crude-exports-hit-record-value-as-oil-price-soared)
  * [South Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Korea "South Korea") secures imports of 273 million barrels of crude oil (by the end of the year) and 2.1 million tons of [naphtha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naphtha "Naphtha") through alternative routes that bypass the Strait of Hormuz, providing several months of supply amid disruptions linked to the Iran war. [(AFP via SpaceWar.com)](https://www.spacewar.com/afp/260415062534.k3fux5ih.html)
