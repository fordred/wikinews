<ul><li><a href="/wiki/2025_Gaza_war_ceasefire" title="2025 Gaza war ceasefire">2025 Gaza war ceasefire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gaza_war_hostage_crisis" title="Gaza war hostage crisis">Gaza war hostage crisis</a>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> says that forensic testing revealed one of the bodies returned by <a href="/wiki/Hamas" title="Hamas">Hamas</a> yesterday does not match with <a href="/wiki/Kidnapping_of_the_Bibas_family" title="Kidnapping of the Bibas family">Shiri Bibas</a> nor with any other hostages. Her sons Ariel and Kfir, as well as <a href="/wiki/Oded_Lifshitz" title="Oded Lifshitz">Oded Lifshitz</a>, were all positively identified. Israel accuses Hamas of violating the ceasefire and demands the return of her body along with the other remaining hostages. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cjry3jzedl1o">(BBC News)</a></li>
<li>Hamas claims that Shiri's body was "mistakenly mixed" with others who were killed and buried under the rubble in <a href="/wiki/Gaza_Strip" title="Gaza Strip">Gaza</a>, and returns an additional body the group says is hers. The identity is soon afterwards positively confirmed by Israel. <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://english.alarabiya.net/News/middle-east/2025/02/21/hamas-says-shiri-bibas-remains-mixed-with-other-human-remains-after-israeli-airstrike">(Al Arabiya)</a> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cjw20d3l4jpo">(BBC News)</a></li></ul></li></ul></li></ul>
