#### Health and environment

* The [U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U.S._National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration "U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration") records the [Arctic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arctic "Arctic")'s [warmest year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_of_the_Arctic "Climate of the Arctic") since measurements began, with average temperatures from October 2024 to September 2025 standing 1.60 °C (2.88 °F) above [the 1991–2020 baseline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climatological_normal "Climatological normal"). [(AFP via RFI)](https://www.rfi.fr/en/international-news/20251216-arctic-sees-unprecedented-heat-as-climate-impacts-cascade)
