* [Politics of Solomon Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics_of_Solomon_Islands "Politics of Solomon Islands")
  * The [parliament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Parliament_of_Solomon_Islands "National Parliament of Solomon Islands") of the [Solomon Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solomon_Islands "Solomon Islands") debates a [no-confidence motion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/No-confidence_motion "No-confidence motion") against [prime minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Solomon_Islands "Prime Minister of Solomon Islands") [Jeremiah Manele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeremiah_Manele "Jeremiah Manele"). The debate is a culmination of a [political crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_crisis "Political crisis") that started with mass resignations of the cabinet on 16 March, recently escalated by a ruling on 1 May by the [Court of Appeal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judiciary_of_Solomon_Islands "Judiciary of Solomon Islands") ordering the reconvening of Parliament. [(RNZ)](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/pacific/594356/manele-calls-parliament-for-thursday-to-face-no-confidence-motion)
  * Manele is ousted from the premiership following a successful no-confidence motion of 26–22, with two [MPs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Member_of_parliament "Member of parliament") absent. Manele remains as [caretaker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caretaker_government "Caretaker government") prime minister until [governor-general](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Governor-General_of_Solomon_Islands "Governor-General of Solomon Islands") [David Tiva Kapu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Tiva_Kapu "David Tiva Kapu") declares a new prime minister. [(Reuters)](https://www.reuters.com/world/china/solomon-islands-pm-ousted-after-losing-confidence-vote-2026-05-07/)
