#### Arts and culture

* [Ireland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland "Republic of Ireland") launches a permanent [basic-income](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_basic_income "Universal basic income") programme for [the arts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Ireland "Culture of Ireland") that pays 2,000 [creative workers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creative_industries "Creative industries") [€](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euro "Euro")325 ([US$](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar "United States dollar")387) per week for three years, following a [government trial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_the_33rd_D%C3%A1il "Government of the 33rd Dáil") that began in 2022, and which [culture minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minister_for_Culture%2C_Communications_and_Sport "Minister for Culture, Communications and Sport") [Patrick O'Donovan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrick_O%27Donovan "Patrick O'Donovan") describes as the first permanent scheme of its kind worldwide. [(Reuters)](https://www.reuters.com/world/ireland-rolls-out-pioneering-basic-income-scheme-artists-2026-02-10/)

#### Law and crime

* [Corruption in Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_Malaysia "Corruption in Malaysia")
  * The [Malaysian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysia "Malaysia") [federal court](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Court_of_Malaysia "Federal Court of Malaysia") reinstates the [bribery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bribery "Bribery") conviction and six-year [prison](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sungai_Buloh_Prison "Sungai Buloh Prison") sentence of former [Federal Land Development Authority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Land_Development_Authority "Federal Land Development Authority") chairperson [Mohd Isa Abdul Samad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohd_Isa_Abdul_Samad "Mohd Isa Abdul Samad") after overturning a 2024 acquittal on nine corruption counts linked to a hotel purchase. * [Recognition of same-sex unions in the Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recognition_of_same-sex_unions_in_the_Philippines "Recognition of same-sex unions in the Philippines")
  * The [Philippine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines") [supreme court](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_Philippines "Supreme Court of the Philippines") rules that [same-sex partners](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Same-sex_relationship "Same-sex relationship") may qualify as co-owners of property under the [Family Code](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Family_Code_of_the_Philippines "Family Code of the Philippines") when both contribute to its acquisition, reversing [lower-court](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judiciary_of_the_Philippines#Regular_courts "Judiciary of the Philippines") decisions in a dispute between two former partners in [Metro Manila](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metro_Manila "Metro Manila"). [(BBC News)](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c70nwz7z7r1o)

#### Politics and elections

* [Elections in Zimbabwe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elections_in_Zimbabwe "Elections in Zimbabwe")
  * [Zimbabwean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimbabwe "Zimbabwe") [president](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Zimbabwe "President of Zimbabwe") [Emmerson Mnangagwa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmerson_Mnangagwa "Emmerson Mnangagwa")'s [cabinet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Third_Cabinet_of_Emmerson_Mnangagwa "Third Cabinet of Emmerson Mnangagwa") approves draft [constitutional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_Zimbabwe "Constitution of Zimbabwe") [amendments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_amendment "Constitutional amendment") that extend presidential [term limits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Term_limit "Term limit") from five to seven years and replace [direct presidential elections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct_election "Direct election") with [parliamentary selection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indirect_election "Indirect election"), which would allow Mnangagwa to remain in office until 2030 if adopted by [parliament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliament_of_Zimbabwe "Parliament of Zimbabwe"). [(Reuters)](https://www.reuters.com/world/africa/zimbabwe-cabinet-backs-bill-that-would-extend-mnangagwas-rule-till-2030-2026-02-10/)
