#### Armed conflicts and attacks

* [Gaza war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_war "Gaza war")
  * [Israeli blockade of the Gaza Strip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israeli_blockade_of_the_Gaza_Strip_%282023%E2%80%93present%29 "Israeli blockade of the Gaza Strip (2023–present)"), [Gaza Strip famine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_Strip_famine "Gaza Strip famine"), [Gaza genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_genocide "Gaza genocide")
    * [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel "Israel") announces the suspension of many international [humanitarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humanitarian_aid "Humanitarian aid") organizations, including [Doctors Without Borders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doctors_Without_Borders "Doctors Without Borders"), the [International Rescue Committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Rescue_Committee "International Rescue Committee"), and divisions of [Oxfam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxfam "Oxfam") and [Caritas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caritas_Internationalis "Caritas Internationalis"), from operating in [Gaza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_Strip "Gaza Strip") starting January 1 for allegedly failing to pass new rules to vet the organizations and cooperating with [Hamas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamas "Hamas") and other [militant groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestinian_Joint_Operations_Room "Palestinian Joint Operations Room"). [(AP)](https://apnews.com/article/gaza-israel-hamas-palestinians-ceasefire-war-news-286106d7a5a911704cdcf1c93248e502)
* [Yemeni civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemeni_civil_war_%282014%E2%80%93present%29 "Yemeni civil war (2014–present)")
  * [2025 Southern Yemen offensive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Southern_Yemen_offensive "2025 Southern Yemen offensive"), [Saudi-led intervention in the Yemeni civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi-led_intervention_in_the_Yemeni_civil_war "Saudi-led intervention in the Yemeni civil war"), [Saudi Arabia–United Arab Emirates relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia%E2%80%93United_Arab_Emirates_relations "Saudi Arabia–United Arab Emirates relations")
    * [Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia "Saudi Arabia") bombs the port of [Mukalla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mukalla "Mukalla"), [Yemen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemen "Yemen"), targeting a ship with weapons delivered from the [United Arab Emirates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates "United Arab Emirates") (UAE) to the [Southern Transitional Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Transitional_Council "Southern Transitional Council"). [(AP)](https://apnews.com/article/saudi-arabia-bomb-yemen-mukalla-weapons-uae-9fc56e4678a12f56d61b1ecf855d4a4e)
  * Yemen ends its defense pact with the [UAE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UAE "UAE"), with [leader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidential_Leadership_Council "Presidential Leadership Council") [Rashad al-Alimi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashad_al-Alimi "Rashad al-Alimi") ordering all [Emirati forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates_Armed_Forces "United Arab Emirates Armed Forces") to leave within 24 hours. He also declares a 90-day national [state of emergency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_of_emergency "State of emergency") and a three-day [blockade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blockade "Blockade") on the country's southern and eastern [regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Governorates_of_Yemen "Governorates of Yemen"). The UAE later announces that its remaining forces in Yemen will leave the country. [(Reuters)](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/saudi-led-coalition-yemen-calls-civilians-mukalla-port-evacuate-saudi-state-news-2025-12-30/) [(Morocco World News)](https://www.moroccoworldnews.com/2025/12/273362/yemen-orders-uae-forces-to-leave-amid-rising-tensions-with-saudi-arabia/)

#### Business and economy

* [Moody's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moody%27s_Ratings "Moody's Ratings") downgrades the [credit rating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Credit_rating "Credit rating") of [Budapest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budapest "Budapest"), [Hungary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungary "Hungary"), to Ba1 and places it on review for a further cut, citing weak [liquidity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funding_liquidity "Funding liquidity"), uncertainty over state transfers, rising [solidarity tax](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tax_rates_in_Europe "Tax rates in Europe") obligations, and [the partial suspension](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rule_of_Law_Conditionality_Regulation "Rule of Law Conditionality Regulation") of [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union") funds to Hungary. [(Reuters)](https://www.reuters.com/world/moodys-cuts-budapests-rating-junk-due-row-with-national-government-2025-12-30/)

#### Disasters and accidents

* [Search for Malaysia Airlines Flight 370](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Search_for_Malaysia_Airlines_Flight_370 "Search for Malaysia Airlines Flight 370")
  * The search for the wreckage of [Malaysia Airlines Flight 370](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysia_Airlines_Flight_370 "Malaysia Airlines Flight 370") resumes in the southern [Indian Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean "Indian Ocean") under a [contingent fee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contingent_fee "Contingent fee") agreement between [Ocean Infinity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocean_Infinity "Ocean Infinity"), which has deployed [autonomous underwater vehicles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autonomous_underwater_vehicle "Autonomous underwater vehicle"), and the [Malaysian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysia "Malaysia") [government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_Malaysia "Government of Malaysia"). [(AFP via France 24)](https://www.france24.com/en/asia-pacific/20251230-mh370-search-resumes-in-indian-ocean-years-after-plane-disappearance)
* [2025 Ollantaytambo District train collision](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Ollantaytambo_District_train_collision "2025 Ollantaytambo District train collision")
  * A [head-on crash](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head-on_collision "Head-on collision") between two trains on the [rail line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rail_transport_in_Peru "Rail transport in Peru") connecting [Cusco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cusco "Cusco") to [Machu Picchu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machu_Picchu "Machu Picchu") in [Urubamba province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urubamba_province "Urubamba province"), [Peru](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peru "Peru"), kills one of the drivers and injures 40 others. [(Al Jazeera)](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/12/31/train-crash-near-perus-machu-picchu-kills-driver-injures-dozens) [(CBS News)](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/train-collision-near-machu-picchu-peru/)
* [High-speed rail](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High-speed_rail "High-speed rail") service [Eurostar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurostar "Eurostar") temporarily suspends all trains between [London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London "London") and [Amsterdam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amsterdam "Amsterdam"), [Brussels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brussels "Brussels"), and [Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris "Paris") due to an "overhead power supply issue" in the [Channel Tunnel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Channel_Tunnel "Channel Tunnel") that caused major disruptions. [(DW)](https://www.dw.com/en/eurostar-urges-travelers-to-postpone-trips-flags-major-disruption/a-75342682)
