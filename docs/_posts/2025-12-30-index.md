#### Armed conflicts and attacks

* [Gaza war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_war "Gaza war")
  * [Israeli blockade of the Gaza Strip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israeli_blockade_of_the_Gaza_Strip_%282023%E2%80%93present%29 "Israeli blockade of the Gaza Strip (2023–present)"), [Gaza Strip famine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_Strip_famine "Gaza Strip famine")
    * [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel "Israel") announces the suspension of many international [humanitarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humanitarian_aid "Humanitarian aid") organizations, including [Doctors Without Borders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doctors_Without_Borders "Doctors Without Borders"), the [International Rescue Committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Rescue_Committee "International Rescue Committee"), and divisions of [Oxfam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxfam "Oxfam") and [Caritas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caritas_Internationalis "Caritas Internationalis"), from operating in [Gaza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_Strip "Gaza Strip") starting January 1 for allegedly failing to pass new rules to vet the organizations and cooperating with [Hamas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamas "Hamas") and other [militant groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestinian_Joint_Operations_Room "Palestinian Joint Operations Room"). [(AP via KUNR)](https://www.kunr.org/world-headlines/2025-12-30/israel-says-it-will-bar-aid-groups-including-doctors-without-borders-from-gaza)
* [Yemeni civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemeni_civil_war_%282014%E2%80%93present%29 "Yemeni civil war (2014–present)")
  * [2025 Southern Yemen offensive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Southern_Yemen_offensive "2025 Southern Yemen offensive"), [Saudi-led intervention in the Yemeni civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi-led_intervention_in_the_Yemeni_civil_war "Saudi-led intervention in the Yemeni civil war"), [Saudi Arabia–United Arab Emirates relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia%E2%80%93United_Arab_Emirates_relations "Saudi Arabia–United Arab Emirates relations")
    * [Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia "Saudi Arabia") bombs the port of [Mukalla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mukalla "Mukalla"), [Yemen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemen "Yemen"), targeting a ship with weapons delivered from the [United Arab Emirates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates "United Arab Emirates") (UAE) to the [Southern Transitional Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Transitional_Council "Southern Transitional Council"). [(AP)](https://apnews.com/article/saudi-arabia-bomb-yemen-mukalla-weapons-uae-9fc56e4678a12f56d61b1ecf855d4a4e)
  * Yemen ends its defense pact with the [UAE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UAE "UAE"), with leader [Rashad al-Alimi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashad_al-Alimi "Rashad al-Alimi") ordering all [Emirati forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates_Armed_Forces "United Arab Emirates Armed Forces") to leave within 24 hours. He also declares a 90-day national [state of emergency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_of_emergency "State of emergency") and a three-day blockade on the country's southern and eastern [regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Governorates_of_Yemen "Governorates of Yemen"). The UAE later announces that its remaining forces in Yemen will leave the country.
