#### Law and crime

* [2025 Taipei Metro knife attack](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Taipei_Metro_knife_attack "2025 Taipei Metro knife attack")
  * Three people are killed and five more injured in a [mass stabbing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_stabbing "Mass stabbing") near a [subway station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metro_station "Metro station") in [Taipei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taipei "Taipei"), [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan"). The perpetrator, who has also threw [smoke bombs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smoke_bomb "Smoke bomb") at the train station, dies during the chase with [police](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Police_Agency_%28Taiwan%29 "National Police Agency (Taiwan)"). [(Reuters)](https://www.reuters.com/world/china/several-injured-attack-taipei-media-reports-2025-12-19/)
* [Caning in Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caning_in_Singapore "Caning in Singapore")
  * The [Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_Singapore "Government of Singapore") of [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore "Singapore") announces that [scammers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scam "Scam") will face mandatory [caning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caning "Caning") of up to 24 strokes from December 30 under changes to the [criminal law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Criminal_law_of_Singapore "Criminal law of Singapore") passed by [Parliament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliament_of_Singapore "Parliament of Singapore") in November. [(AP)](https://apnews.com/article/singapore-caning-scam-law-4f12fbbf443ba7ec2227c4800ca1efa0)
* [December 2025 Bangladesh violence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/December_2025_Bangladesh_violence "December 2025 Bangladesh violence")
  * Protests and [riots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riot "Riot") erupts across [Bangladesh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh "Bangladesh") after the [assassination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assassination "Assassination") of activist [Osman Hadi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Osman_Hadi "Osman Hadi") by two members of the banned [Awami League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Awami_League "Awami League") party. [(Al Jazeera)](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2025/12/19/live-violence-breaks-out-in-bangladesh-after-prominent-protest-leader-dies)
