#### Health and environment

* [Environmental policy of the second Trump administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_policy_of_the_second_Trump_administration "Environmental policy of the second Trump administration")
  * [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States") [president](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_the_United_States "President of the United States") [Donald Trump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Trump "Donald Trump") revokes the [Environmental Protection Agency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Environmental_Protection_Agency "United States Environmental Protection Agency")'s [2009 endangerment finding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulation_of_greenhouse_gases_under_the_Clean_Air_Act "Regulation of greenhouse gases under the Clean Air Act"), which classified [greenhouse gases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas "Greenhouse gas") as a threat to [public health](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_in_the_United_States "Health in the United States") and formed the legal basis for [federal climate regulations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_policy_of_the_United_States "Climate change policy of the United States"), and concurrently rescinds [vehicle emission standards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_vehicle_emission_standards "United States vehicle emission standards"). [(AFP via ABS-CBN)](https://www.abs-cbn.com/news/world/2026/2/13/trump-dismantles-legal-basis-for-us-climate-rules-0653)
* [Greenhouse gas emissions by China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions_by_China "Greenhouse gas emissions by China")
  * The [Centre for Research on Energy and Clean Air](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Centre_for_Research_on_Energy_and_Clean_Air "Centre for Research on Energy and Clean Air") reports that [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China")'s [carbon dioxide emissions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions "Greenhouse gas emissions") remained flat or declined slightly in 2025 by about 0.3%, marking the first full calendar year in which emissions in China did not rise despite increasing [energy demand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electricity_sector_in_China "Electricity sector in China"), largely due to expanded [renewable energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renewable_energy_in_China "Renewable energy in China") capacity. [(AFP via France 24)](https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20260212-china-carbon-emissions-flat-or-falling-in-2025-analysis)
