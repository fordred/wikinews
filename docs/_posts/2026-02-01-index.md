#### Sports

* [2026 Australian Open](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Australian_Open "2026 Australian Open")
  * In [tennis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tennis "Tennis"), [Carlos Alcaraz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carlos_Alcaraz "Carlos Alcaraz") of [Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tennis_in_Spain "Tennis in Spain") wins his first [Australian Open](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australian_Open "Australian Open") title and becomes the youngest man to complete the [career](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Grand_Slam_men%27s_singles_champions "List of Grand Slam men's singles champions") [Grand Slam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Slam_%28tennis%29 "Grand Slam (tennis)") in [singles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singles_%28tennis%29 "Singles (tennis)") after defeating [Novak Djokovic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Novak_Djokovic "Novak Djokovic") of [Serbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sport_in_Serbia "Sport in Serbia") in four [sets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Set_%28tennis%29 "Set (tennis)") of the [men's singles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Australian_Open_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_singles "2026 Australian Open – Men's singles") final, 2–6, 6–2, 6–3, 7–5. [(AFP via ABS-CBN)](https://www.abs-cbn.com/sports/othersports/2026/2/1/tennis-alcaraz-beats-djokovic-to-win-maiden-australian-open-title-1959)
