#### Politics and elections

* [2026 Peruvian general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Peruvian_general_election "2026 Peruvian general election")
  * [Peruvians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peruvians "Peruvians") vote to elect 130 members of the [Chamber of Deputies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congress_of_the_Republic_of_Peru "Congress of the Republic of Peru") and 60 members of a newly created [senate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senate "Senate"), while also determining the top two [presidential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Peru "President of Peru") candidates that will advance to a [run-off election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Run-off_election "Run-off election"). [(AP)](https://apnews.com/article/peru-presidential-election-crime-fujimori-aliaga-980a5ee78d1fed794dc6c9b90f9a1ce4)
  * [Peruvian police](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Police_of_Peru "National Police of Peru") and prosecutors conduct searches at the [electoral authority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Office_of_Electoral_Processes "National Office of Electoral Processes")'s headquarters and a private company as part of an investigation into delays in delivering voting materials that prevent more than 60,000 people from voting. Officials attribute the issue to logistical failures affecting multiple polling stations in [Lima](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lima "Lima"). The electoral authority later announces that some polling stations will reopen. [(AFP via *Free Malaysia Today*)](https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/world/2026/04/13/perus-police-investigators-raid-election-hq-amid-vote-delays) [(AFP via NAMPA)](https://www.nampa.org/text/22908458)
  * Exit polls show [Popular Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popular_Force "Popular Force")'s [Keiko Fujimori](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keiko_Fujimori "Keiko Fujimori") leading the presidential election with about 16 percent of the vote, with no candidate securing a majority and a run-off expected between Fujimori and [Popular Renewal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popular_Renewal "Popular Renewal")'s [Rafael López Aliaga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rafael_L%C3%B3pez_Aliaga "Rafael López Aliaga"). [(AFP with *Le Monde*)](https://www.lemonde.fr/en/international/article/2026/04/13/peru-exit-polls-show-fujimori-ahead-runoff-likely_6752352_4.html)

* [2026 Hungarian parliamentary election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Hungarian_parliamentary_election "2026 Hungarian parliamentary election")
  * [Hungarians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarians "Hungarians") vote to elect all 199 members of the [National Assembly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Assembly_%28Hungary%29 "National Assembly (Hungary)"). The incumbent [prime minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Hungary "Prime Minister of Hungary"), [Viktor Orbán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viktor_Orb%C3%A1n "Viktor Orbán"), who was seeking a fifth consecutive term in office, [concedes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concession_%28politics%29 "Concession (politics)") defeat and calls [Tisza Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tisza_Party "Tisza Party") leader [Péter Magyar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/P%C3%A9ter_Magyar "Péter Magyar") on the telephone to congratulate him on his victory. The [pro-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pro-Europeanism "Pro-Europeanism") Tisza Party wins two-thirds of the seats in Hungary's legislature. The turnout is the highest since the [fall of communism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/End_of_communism_in_Hungary "End of communism in Hungary"), at 77.8%. [(BBC)](https://www.bbc.com/news/live/c2d8zw2d3rkt) [(*The Guardian*)](https://www.theguardian.com/world/live/2026/apr/12/hungary-election-latest-results-viktor-orban-peter-magyar-fidesz-tisza-russia-europe-live-news-updates#maincontent)
